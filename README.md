# California National Parks Data Analysis

This project analyzes visitation patterns across California National Parks to understand why some parks receive significantly more visitors than others.

The analysis explores relationships between park area, geolocation, and funding to determine factors that may influence park popularity and visitor numbers.

## Research Question
How can California National Parks increase visitation and improve resource allocation using data?

## Tools Used
- Wolfram Mathematica
- Data Visualization
- Exploratory Data Analysis

## Dataset
The dataset contains information on U.S. National Parks including park area, visitation statistics, budgets, and staffing data.

## Analysis Focus
The project examines three key factors:

• **Area vs Visitation** – Larger parks may have underutilized space or capacity.  
• **Geolocation** – Parks closer to major cities often receive more visitors.  
• **Budget vs Visitors** – Parks with lower funding may struggle to maintain visitor infrastructure.

## Key Examples
The analysis highlights parks such as:

- Yosemite
- Joshua Tree
- Death Valley

These parks vary greatly in area and visitor numbers, providing insight into how location and funding influence visitation.
